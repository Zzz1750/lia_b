The Shadow on Blackthorn Road

from Toad Series


Blackthorn Road was infamous in the small, mist-shrouded town of Hollow Creek. Locals whispered about the stretch of road that snaked through the dense, ancient forest. No one dared tread there after dark—not since the disappearances began fifty years ago.

It was said that a woman named Eleanor Sinclair had vanished on that very road. She was a solitary figure who lived in a grand, decaying mansion deep within the woods. The night she disappeared, a storm had rolled in, the kind that rattled windows and silenced even the bravest of townsfolk. Her carriage was found overturned, her driver dead, but Eleanor was never seen again. Rumors spread that she’d been taken by something unnatural.

Decades later, Thomas, a skeptic and journalist with a thirst for debunking myths, decided to investigate the legend for an exposé. Armed with a flashlight, a notebook, and a sense of bravado, he ventured onto Blackthorn Road just as the sun dipped below the horizon. The air felt unnaturally cold, and the trees loomed like silent sentinels.

As Thomas walked, he felt an odd sense of being watched. The shadows seemed to shift when he wasn’t looking, and the sound of rustling leaves seemed to follow him. Dismissing it as his imagination, he pressed on.
